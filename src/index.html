Frequently Asked Questions

  • You should visit the dentist at least twice a year for a routine check-up and cleaning. However, if you have specific dental issues like gum disease or frequent cavities, we may recommend more frequent visits to ensure your oral health stays on track.

  • The best way to prevent cavities is by brushing your teeth twice a day with fluoride toothpaste, flossing daily, and limiting sugary or acidic foods and drinks. Regular dental check-ups and cleanings also help remove plaque buildup that can lead to cavities.

  • Yes! Professional teeth whitening is safe when done correctly. In-office whitening treatments and dentist-approved take-home kits use safe levels of bleaching agents that won’t harm your enamel. However, overuse of store-bought whitening products may cause tooth sensitivity or gum irritation.

  • Early signs of gum disease include red, swollen, or bleeding gums, persistent bad breath, and gum recession. If left untreated, it can progress to more severe stages, potentially leading to tooth loss. Regular dental cleanings and good oral hygiene can help prevent and manage gum disease.

  • Not everyone needs to have their wisdom teeth removed. However, if they are impacted, causing pain, crowding, or increasing the risk of infection, extraction may be recommended. A dental exam and X-rays will help determine whether removal is necessary in your case.
